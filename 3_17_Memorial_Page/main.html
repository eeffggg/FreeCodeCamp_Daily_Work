Dr. Yuan Longping

The man who saved hundreds of millions of lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Yuan's life:

  • Born in Beijing, China (on September 7)
  • Enters Southwest Agricultural College (now part of Southwest University) to study agriculture
  • Witnesses severe famine in China, which sparks his determination to develop high-yield rice varieties
  • Begins research on hybrid rice, starting his decades-long exploration of heterosis in rice
  • Successfully develops the first high-yielding three-line hybrid rice, marking a breakthrough in rice breeding
  • Establishes a large-scale hybrid rice production system, promoting the new variety across China (commercial production began around 1976)
  • Achieves the two-line hybrid rice technology, further boosting rice yields
  • Develops the super hybrid rice with a yield target of 10.5 tons per hectare (later super hybrid varieties achieved even higher yields, with records exceeding 17 tons per hectare in demonstration fields)
  • Wins the World Food Prize (jointly with Monty P. Jones) for his outstanding contributions to global food security
  • His hybrid rice technology is widely adopted in dozens of countries and regions (estimates suggest over 40–60 countries/regions over time), helping to alleviate hunger worldwide
  • Passes away at the age of 90 (on May 22), leaving behind a legacy of feeding billions
